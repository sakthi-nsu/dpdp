What is the DPDP Act?

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 is India's data protection law designed to regulate the collection, storage, processing, and sharing of personal data.

Who must comply?
  • All businesses operating in India.
  • Organizations offering goods or services to individuals in India.
  • Startups, MSMEs, and Enterprises alike.

Why DPDPA Compliance Matters

With digital transformation expanding, the DPDP Act mandates strict data protection practices. A clear compliance framework minimizes legal exposure and builds customer trust.

Non-compliance leads to heavy fines and reputational risks. Secure your data governance today to stay ahead.

Frequently Asked Questions

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 is India's comprehensive data protection legislation that governs the processing of digital personal data.

Organizations have until 13 May 2027 to fully comply with the DPDPA rules.

Valid consent under DPDPA must be free, informed, specific, clear, prior to processing, and easily withdrawable.

Fiduciaries must notify the Data Protection Board and affected individuals.

Data Principals have the right to access, correct, delete, and port their data. They also have the right to object to processing and withdraw consent.

A child, according to DPDPA, is any individual who has not yet reached 18 years of age.

Organizations must maintain records of consent, data processing, breaches, sharing, DPIAs, complaints, and policy updates for DPDP compliance.

Companies should update their privacy policies under DPDP to clearly explain data collection, processing, consent, rights, retention, and contact details.

The DPDPA complements the IT Act 2000 by providing a dedicated framework for personal data protection and privacy in India.
