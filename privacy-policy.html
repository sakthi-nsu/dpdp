Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 27, 2026

1. Introduction

NSU Secure Solutions ("we", "us", or "our") operates the website dpdpatn.com and the DPDPA TN platform (collectively, the "Services"). We are committed to protecting your personal data in compliance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDPA) and the DPDP Rules, 2025.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, store, and protect personal data when you visit our website, use our resources (rules summaries, templates, checklists), contact us, or subscribe to updates.

2. Personal Data We Collect

We collect only the minimum personal data necessary to provide our Services:

  • Contact & Identification Data: Name, email address, phone number (when you fill forms, subscribe, or contact us).
  • Communication Data: Messages, queries, or feedback you send via contact form, email, or WhatsApp.
  • Technical Data: IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited, time spent (collected via cookies & analytics — see Section 7).
  • Subscription Data: Email for newsletter updates on DPDPA developments.

We do not collect sensitive personal data (health, biometric, financial, etc.) unless explicitly provided by you in a query.

3. Purpose & Lawful Basis

We process your personal data only for specific, legitimate purposes under DPDPA:

  • To respond to your inquiries, provide advisory support, or share DPDPA resources (consent / legitimate interest).
  • To send optional newsletter updates on rules, enforcement, and compliance tips (explicit consent).
  • To improve website functionality, security, and user experience (legitimate interest).
  • To comply with legal obligations (e.g., grievance redressal records, audit trails).

4. Data Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell your personal data. We may share it only:

  • With service providers (hosting, email tools, analytics) under strict processor agreements compliant with DPDPA.
  • If required by law, court order, or government authority.
  • In case of merger, acquisition, or asset sale (with notice where feasible).

No international transfers occur unless necessary and protected by appropriate safeguards.

5. Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary:

  • Contact/query data: Up to 3 years after last interaction (or longer if required for legal defense).
  • Newsletter subscribers: Until you unsubscribe or request deletion.
  • Technical logs: Up to 12 months for security purposes.

After retention period, data is securely deleted or anonymized.

6. Your Rights under DPDPA

As a Data Principal, you have the right to:

  • Access, correct, or erase your personal data
  • Withdraw consent (where processing is consent-based)
  • Nominate a representative
  • Grieve / complain about processing

To exercise any right, email us at grievance@dpdpatn.com. We respond within the timelines prescribed under DPDPA Rules.

7. Cookies & Analytics

We use essential cookies for site functionality and limited analytics (Google Analytics / similar) to understand usage patterns. No advertising cookies or trackers are used. You can manage cookies via your browser settings.

8. Security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures (encryption, access controls, regular VAPT testing) to protect your data. However, no internet transmission is 100% secure — we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in law, our practices, or Services. The updated version will be posted here with a revised "Last Updated" date. Significant changes will be notified via email or site notice.

Questions about this Privacy Policy? Contact our Grievance Officer at grievance@dpdpatn.com.
