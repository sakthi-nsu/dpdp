Data Retention Policy

Effective Date: [Insert Date] Last Revised: [Insert Date]

1.1 Introduction

This Data Retention Policy ("Policy") is established by [Insert Organisation Name] to ensure the lawful, fair, and transparent handling of personal data in compliance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDPA"), the Information Technology Act, 2000, and other applicable laws and regulations. This Policy sets out the principles and procedures governing the retention and secure disposal of data processed by the Organisation.

1.2 Objectives

  • Ensure compliance with legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations related to data retention.
  • Protect the privacy and security of personal data throughout its lifecycle.
  • Provide clear guidelines for the retention, archiving, and destruction of data.
  • Minimise risks associated with over-retention, such as legal liabilities and security breaches.

1.3 Scope

This Policy applies to:

  • Data Types: Personal data, sensitive personal data, business records, operational data, and other information processed by the Organisation, regardless of format (electronic or physical).
  • Entities Covered: All employees, contractors, vendors, and third parties processing data on behalf of the Organisation.
  • Systems: Applications, databases, storage media, and systems used by the Organisation.

1.4 Data Retention Principles

  • Purpose Limitation: Retain data only for as long as necessary to fulfill its original purpose, as outlined in privacy notices or agreements.
  • Retention Periods: Retention periods are based on legal, regulatory, contractual, and operational requirements, as detailed in the Data Retention Schedule (Annexure A).
  • Data Minimisation: Retain only data necessary for specific purposes.
  • Access Controls: Limit access to retained data to authorised personnel.
  • Secure Disposal: Ensure secure destruction or anonymisation of data no longer required.

1.5 Responsibilities

1. Data Protection Officer (DPO):

  • Oversee Policy compliance and periodic reviews of the Data Retention Schedule.
  • Address queries related to data retention and disposal.

2. Department Heads:

  • Ensure departmental compliance with the Policy.
  • Identify and classify data within their areas of responsibility.

3. Employees and Contractors:

  • Adhere to data retention guidelines.
  • Report non-compliance or data incidents to the DPO.

1.6 Data Retention Schedule

The Data Retention Schedule (Annexure A) specifies:

  • Categories of data.
  • Retention periods for each category.
  • Legal or regulatory justification for retention.
  • Procedures for data archiving, retrieval, and secure disposal.

1.7 Data Destruction and Disposal

1. Disposal Methods:

  • Physical Records: Shredded, incinerated, or securely destroyed.
  • Electronic Data: Permanently deleted using industry-standard techniques.

2. Destruction Documentation:

  • Maintain records of data destruction, including the date, method, and responsible personnel.

1.8 Audit and Monitoring

  • Internal Audits: Conduct regular audits to ensure adherence to the Policy and Data Retention Schedule.
  • Non-Compliance: Report instances of non-compliance to the DPO and address them promptly, with potential disciplinary actions.
  • Policy Reviews: Review the Policy annually or as required by legal or operational changes.

1.9 Exceptions

Any exceptions to retention or disposal requirements must be:

  • Approved by the DPO.
  • Documented with a rationale for the exception.

1.10 Contact Information

For questions or concerns regarding this Policy, contact:

Data Protection Officer: [Insert Name]

Email: [Insert Email Address]

Phone: [Insert Phone Number]

Address: [Insert Organisation Address]

1.11 Acknowledgement and Acceptance

All employees, contractors, and third parties processing data on behalf of the Organisation must acknowledge and accept this Policy as part of their responsibilities.

Approved by: [Insert Approver Name]

Title: [Insert Approver Title]

Employee Data Protection Policy

Effective Date: [Insert Date] Last Revised: [Insert Date]

1.1 Introduction

This Employee Data Protection Policy ("Policy") is established by [Company Name] ("Company") to ensure the lawful, fair, and transparent processing of employee personal data in compliance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDP Act") and other applicable laws. The Company is committed to safeguarding employee privacy and ensuring data protection while fulfilling legal and operational requirements.

1.2 Objectives

  • Ensure compliance with the DPDP Act and related regulations.
  • Protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of employee personal data.
  • Provide guidelines for the collection, use, storage, and secure disposal of employee personal data.
  • Outline the rights of employees regarding their personal data and the Company's obligations.

1.3 Scope

This Policy applies to:

  • Individuals: Current, former, and prospective employees, interns, contractors, consultants, and temporary workers.
  • Data Types: Personal and sensitive personal data processed in any format (electronic, physical, or verbal).
  • Processing Activities: All activities related to the collection, use, storage, sharing, and deletion of employee personal data.

1.4 Definitions

  • Personal Data: Information identifying an individual, such as name, address, contact details, and identification numbers.
  • Sensitive Personal Data: Data related to health, financial status, biometrics, or other categories defined under the DPDP Act.
  • Processing: Any operation on personal data, including collection, recording, storage, sharing, or deletion
  • Data Subject: An employee whose personal data is processed.
  • Data Protection Officer (DPO): The individual responsible for overseeing data protection compliance within the Company.

1.5 Principles of Data Processing

  • Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency: Inform employees about the purpose and use of their data.
  • Purpose Limitation: Collect and use data only for specified, legitimate purposes.
  • Data Minimization: Collect only data necessary for specific purposes.
  • Accuracy: Ensure data is accurate and updated promptly.
  • Storage Limitation: Retain data only as long as necessary for its original purpose.
  • Security: Protect data against unauthorized access, loss, or damage.

1.6 Categories of Data Collected

The Company may collect the following data from employees:

  • Identification & Contact: Name, DOB, employee ID, address, and email.
  • Contact Information: Address, phone number, and email.
  • Employment Information: Job title, department, performance evaluations, and work history.
  • Financial Information: Bank account details, salary information, tax identification numbers.
  • Sensitive Data: Health records, fitness assessments, and biometric data where applicable.

1.7 Data Protection Measures

  • Access Controls: Limit access to employee data to authorized personnel.
  • Encryption: Encrypt data stored electronically to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Physical Security: Store physical records securely in locked cabinets.
  • Breach Management: Implement an incident management framework for prompt response.

1.8 Employee Rights

Employees have the right to access, rectify, or request the erasure of their personal data. They also hold the right to restrict processing, request data portability, and withdraw consent at any time.

Email: [Insert DPO Email Address]

Phone: [Insert DPO Phone Number]

Approved by: [Insert Approver Name] | Title: [Insert Approver Title] | Date: [Insert Approval Date]

Cookie Policy

Effective Date: [Insert Date] Last Revised: [Insert Date]

1.1 Introduction

At [Company Name], we value your privacy and are committed to being transparent about the technologies we use. This Cookie Policy ("Policy") explains how cookies and similar technologies are employed on our website(s) to recognize visitors and analyze interactions with content and advertisements. It is essential that you read this Policy carefully before using our website(s) or services.

For a complete understanding of how we collect, use, and protect your data, we recommend reviewing our Privacy Policy. By continuing to browse or use our website(s) and services, you consent to our use of cookies and similar technologies in accordance with this Policy.

1.2 What are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device to enhance website functionality and user experience. We classify cookies as follows:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for website functionality.
  • Performance Cookies: Used for analytics and improving usability.
  • Marketing Cookies: Used for targeted advertising.

Cookies can either be session-based, lasting only for the duration of your visit, or persistent, remaining on your device for future visits. Cookies may be set directly by the website (first-party) or by external services (third-party).

1.3 Why Do We Use Cookies?

The use of cookies serves several purposes, including:

  • Functionality: Remembering login information to save you from entering it repeatedly.
  • Security: Helping to identify and mitigate potential security threats.
  • Personalization: Understanding user preferences to tailor content and products.
  • Marketing: Serving relevant advertisements and monitoring their effectiveness.
  • Analytics: Conducting performance assessments to improve website usability.

1.4 Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have the right to manage your cookie preferences, including refusing certain types of cookies, by accessing the "Cookie Preferences" tab available on our website(s). You may also withdraw any previously granted consent at any time.

Additionally, you can delete cookies from your device by adjusting your browser settings. Please note that blocking cookies may impact your ability to access certain features or functionalities of our website(s).

1.5 Contact Information

Should you have any questions, concerns, or requests related to this Cookie Policy or the use of cookies on our website(s), please feel free to contact us.

This Privacy Policy ("Policy") explains how [Your Company Name] ("Company," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, shares, and protects personal data obtained through our websites, applications, and related services. It is designed to comply with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDP Act") and other applicable laws. By accessing our website or using our services, you consent to the practices described in this Policy.

2. Scope of the Policy

This Policy applies to:

  • Personal data collected through websites, applications, and online platforms.
  • Data provided by customers, employees, vendors, and business partners.
  • Information gathered during recruitment or operational processes.
  • Automatically collected data via digital platforms.

3. Definitions

  • Personal Data: Information that identifies or can identify an individual, such as name, contact details, and online identifiers.
  • Sensitive Personal Data: Information such as financial data, biometric data, health information, or other data classified as sensitive under applicable laws.

4. Collection of Personal Data

4.1 Direct Collection: Personal data is collected directly during contractual engagements, use of services, or communications through customer service channels.

4.2 Automatic Collection: Data such as device identifiers, IP addresses, geolocation, and user preferences are collected via cookies and similar technologies. Refer to our Cookie Policy for more details.

4.3 Other Sources: We also collect data from business contacts, recruitment applications (educational/professional records), and visitors to our premises (including CCTV footage for security).

5. Purpose and Use of Personal Data

The Company processes personal data for purposes including:

  • Fulfilling contractual obligations and KYC compliance.
  • Complying with legal and regulatory requirements.
  • Improving website functionality and personalizing user experiences.
  • Marketing and promotional communications (with consent).
  • Recruitment, employment, and operational management.

6. Sharing of Personal Data

6.1 Regulatory and Legal Compliance: Personal data may be disclosed to regulatory authorities and law enforcement agencies as required by law.

6.2 Third-Party Service Providers: We share data with trusted service providers for IT support, payment processing, and marketing services. Contracts ensure that data is used only for intended purposes.

6.3 Business Transfers: Personal data may be transferred during mergers, acquisitions, or asset sales.

7. Data Security

We implement robust measures to protect personal data, including:

  • Encryption: AES-256 encryption for sensitive data.
  • Access Controls: Restricted access based on role and necessity.
  • Audits: Regular security assessments and compliance reviews.
  • Incident Response: Procedures to address potential data breaches promptly.

8. Retention of Personal Data

Personal data is retained only as long as necessary to fulfill its purpose or comply with legal obligations. After the retention period, data is securely deleted or anonymized.

9. Children’s Privacy

Our services are not directed at individuals under 18. If you believe we have collected data from a minor, contact us immediately.

10. Your Rights

Under the DPDP Act, you have the following rights:

  • Access & Rectification: Request a copy of your data or correct inaccuracies.
  • Erasure: Request deletion of data no longer required.
  • Withdrawal of Consent: Revoke your consent at any time.

To exercise these rights, contact [Privacy Officer Email Address]. Requests will be processed within [Insert Timeline, e.g., 30 days] or as required by law
